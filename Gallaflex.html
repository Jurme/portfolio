UX CASE STUDY

Reimagining the
Art Gallery Experience

Gallaflex streamlines exhibition discovery and check-ins, helping art enthusiasts immerse themselves without the logistical hassle.

Role

Product Designer

Timeline

4 Weeks Sprints

Platform

iOS & Android

Tools

Figma, Miro

The Challenge

Art enthusiasts often struggle to discover nearby galleries when exploring new cities. On top of discovery, the physical check-in process involves queuing and paper tickets, taking time away from the actual experience.

Our Goal

To design a unified platform that allows users to discover exhibitions based on taste, schedule visits instantly, and access curation details to deepen their understanding of the art.

01. Research

Understanding
the User

We conducted interviews with art lovers to identify friction points in the current gallery visiting experience.

Participant Criteria

  • Art enthusiasts
  • Frequent travelers
  • Age 18-45
1

Discovery is fragmented

Users rely on multiple disjointed sources (Google Maps, Instagram, blogs) to find galleries, leading to decision fatigue.

2

Logistics kill the vibe

Long queues and physical ticketing processes break the immersion before the user even sees the art.

3

Missing Context

Visitors often want more context about the artwork but find gallery plaques insufficient or crowded.

02. Persona

Meet Olivia

We synthesized our findings into a primary persona to guide our design decisions.

Olivia, 28

The Creative Explorer

"I want to immerse myself in the art world without the hassle of logistics."

Goals

  • • Understand art history instantly
  • • Check-in seamlessly via mobile
  • • Find hidden gems nearby

Frustrations

  • • Galleries closed unexpectedly
  • • Paper tickets & long lines
  • • Overcrowded spaces

Says

"I want connection."

Thinks

"Needs convenience."

Does

"Shares on social."

Feels

"Inspired yet annoyed."
